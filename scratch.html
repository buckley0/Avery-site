Why Baseball?

Baseball is more than a sport — it is a mirror held up to American history. From the integration of the leagues to the statistics revolution sparked by Moneyball, baseball has always been a data-driven, story-rich institution unlike any other.

As a Political Science student, I see in baseball the same forces I study every day: strategy, leadership, pressure, and the cold logic of numbers meeting the warm chaos of human performance.

What Makes a Great Player?

Great baseball players are defined by a combination of physical tools and mental acuity. Here is a breakdown of the key attributes — ordered by category, with specific skills nested within each:

  1. Offensive Skills
    • Plate discipline (walk rate, strikeout rate)
    • Contact ability (batting average, hard-hit %)
    • Power (home run rate, slugging percentage)
    • Speed on the bases (stolen base efficiency)
  2. Pitching & Defense
    • Velocity and pitch movement (spin rate)
    • Command and control (walk rate, zone %)
    • Fielding range and arm strength (OAA)
    • Catcher framing and blocking skills
  3. Mental & Intangible Qualities
    • Performance under pressure (WPA — Win Probability Added)
    • Consistent preparation and film study
    • Adaptability across a long 162-game season
  4. Advanced Analytics (Sabermetrics)
    • WAR (Wins Above Replacement) — the gold standard
    • wRC+ (Weighted Runs Created Plus)
    • FIP (Fielding Independent Pitching)
    • BABIP (Batting Average on Balls In Play)

A Brief History of the Game

1869

The Cincinnati Red Stockings become the first openly all-professional baseball team.

1947

Jackie Robinson breaks Major League Baseball's color barrier, joining the Brooklyn Dodgers.

1994

A player's strike cancels the World Series for the first time in 90 years.

2001

Luis Gonzalez's walk-off single in Game 7 gives the Arizona Diamondbacks their first World Series title over the Yankees.

2023

New pitch clock rules dramatically speed up the game, cutting average game time by ~26 minutes.
